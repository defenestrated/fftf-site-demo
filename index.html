In early January, U.S. Secretary of the Interior Ryan Zinke revealed the government's plan to open up "nearly the entire U.S. Continental Shelf" for oil and gas drilling. We've known for a while that this is a terrible idea, and yet the Trump administration continues to ignore reality in order to appease irresponsible oil companies.

It's clear from the language of the DOI's proposal that this plan's goal is not to responsibly develop new energy sources: one of the plan's authors wants America to be "moving from aspiring for energy independence to attaining energy dominance" (Vincent DeVito, Counselor for Energy Policy at the Dept. of the Interior).

photo credit US Coast Guard, via Wikimedia

According to a 2014 study in Alaska by none other than the Dept. of Interior, there's a 75% chance of "one or more large spills occuring" in the 74-year period between exploration and final production. But even if that (somehow) doesn't happen, the drilling operation alone destabilizes entire ecosystems and releases toxic metals like lead, mercury, and cadmium into our seafood supply.

...But spills do happen, and when they do, they're some of the biggest environmental disasters on record. We still don't fully understand the extent of the damage that the BP oil spill on the Deepwater Horizon caused in 2010.
